Garden Care Tips

Water properly

Watering your garden is a good thing, but since many diseases need water just as much as plants do, how you go about it makes a big difference. Many pathogens in the soil and air need water to move, grow, and reproduce.

Apply the correct fertilizer

The decision about whether or not to fertilize should be based on the nutrition requirements needed by your soil conditions. Most plants, trees, and shrubs will benefit from proper fertilization with a slow-release product just before mulch is applied.

Test your soil

Test your soil once every few years to make sure it has the proper pH balance and mix of nutrients. You can usually get your soil tested at your state's local agricultural extension office, or ask your lawn care or landscape professional.

Inspect your irrigation system

Once the weather has warmed up for the year, turn on your irrigation system and inspect it to see if it is in working condition. Our company provides the installation of irrigation systems.
